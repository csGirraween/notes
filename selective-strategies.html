Selective High School Placement Test Information and Strategies

1. Overview of the Selective High School Placement Test

The Selective High School Placement Test is designed to assess the academic capabilities of students seeking entry into selective high schools in NSW. The test evaluates skills in four key areas:

  • Reading: Comprehension and interpretation of written texts.
  • Mathematical Reasoning: Application of mathematical concepts and problem-solving skills.
  • Thinking Skills: Critical thinking, logical reasoning, and problem-solving abilities.
  • Writing: Ability to express ideas effectively in written form.

Each of these components is weighted equally, contributing 25% to the overall placement score.

2. Test Structure and Timing

The test is conducted over a single day and is divided into four sections with the following structure and time allocations:

  • Reading Test: 40 minutes
    • 16 multiple-choice questions (some with multiple parts).
  • Mathematical Reasoning Test: 40 minutes
    • 35 multiple-choice questions.
  • Thinking Skills Test: 40 minutes
    • 40 multiple-choice questions.
  • Writing Test: 30 minutes
    • One writing task based on a given prompt.

The tests are administered in the following order:

  1. Reading Test
  2. Mathematical Reasoning Test
  3. Thinking Skills Test
  4. Writing Test

There are short breaks between each test section.

Strategies for Each Test Component

To maximize your performance, consider the following strategies tailored to each section:

  • Practice Active Reading: Engage with various text types to enhance comprehension skills.
  • Identify Key Ideas: Focus on understanding the main idea of the text and analyse key words from the questions and identify relevant sections from the text.
  • Time Management: Allocate time wisely to read passages and answer questions without rushing. Ideally, read the questions then the text so you have some context of what will be asked and need to check for.
  • Strengthen Fundamentals: Ensure a solid grasp of basic arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and data interpretation.
  • Apply Logical Steps: Break down problems into manageable steps to find solutions systematically.
  • Manage Your Time Wisely!: Track your time! If you spend 1 minute a question that will leave 5 minutes to check over for mistakes.
  • Develop Critical Thinking: Practice the questions regularly. Use the booklets and resources we have given you.
  • Understand key ideas: Practice evaluating statements and identifying assumptions or flaws in reasoning.
  • Stay Calm Under Pressure: Maintain composure to think clearly and avoid hasty decisions. Remember, ideally you want to spend 1 minute a question. (40 min / 40 questions)
  • Understand the Prompt: Ensure your response directly addresses the given topic.
  • Plan Before Writing: Organise thoughts with a brief outline to structure your essay effectively.
  • Think out of the Box: Use unique ideas, great language and cohesive flow.
  • Proofread: Allocate time to proofread and correct errors.

3. Scoring and Placement Process

Each test component contributes equally to the overall placement score, with the following weightings:

  • Reading: 25%
  • Mathematical Reasoning: 25%
  • Thinking Skills: 25%
  • Writing: 25%

Students are ranked based on their scores from all test components. Placement offers are made according to these rankings, with higher-ranked students receiving offers to their preferred selective high schools, subject to availability.
